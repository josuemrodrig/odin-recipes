Menudo

Menudo, also called pancita, mondongo or mole de panza, depending on the state in which it is prepared, is a broth made with the giblets or belly of beef or goat, although the latter animal is used less frequently. This is a typical dish of Mexican culture.

Ingredients

  • 1 kilo of beef belly
  • 1 kilo of beef leg
  • 1 kilo of precooked corn kernels
  • 1 Onion
  • 1 Green chili
  • 1 Head of garlic
  • Lemons
  • Baguette
  • Water, only the necessary
  • Salt
  • 4 Chicken bouillon cubes
  • Fresh coriander

Preparation

  1. In a pot, cook the beef leg with plenty of water
  2. Cut the beef belly (menudo) into not very small squares and wash the corn
  3. Once the water with the beef leg has boiled for about 15 minutes, add the corn
  4. 20 Minutes after you added the corn you can add the menudo (tummy)
  5. Once it is well cooked, add the onion, the head of garlic, the green chili, the salt and the chicken bouillon cubes
  6. Tip: separately, cut the onion and cilantro into small squares finely (with this you will give more flavor to the menudo)
